Life and love go on, let the music play.



Music exists everywhere. But for me, music is more of a simple melody that comforts people. I've played piano for 12 years, and now, I can still see the vast unknown in the music field, awaiting my discovery.

If our lives are pie charts, I would say that Music takes up to 45% of my large cake. Each week, I spend at least 5 hours on practicing for upcoming competitions or presentations. I usually participate in school's annual Christmas concert and many other national piano competitions.

Hi, I'm Ya-Cheng Shieh from Taipei, Taiwan. I started learning to play the piano since 5. To admit, I have similar paths as other pianists: first start off from teachings about how beats work + years of cumbersome practices about the basics of music. However, that stably built up my foundation and appreciation to classical music. Today, I'm proud of my determination on piano and enjoy each presentation or competition I experienced.

Background

I come from a family of elegance and a bookish atmosphere. Both of my parents are fond of classical music, especially soft piano pieces of Chopin or powerful symphonies of Beethoven. Thus, born in this background, I also come to love music since young.

Develop

When I was five, I first witnessed the enthralling performance of Lang Lang. His hands moved swiftly and gracefully along the ivory keys, depicting impressive emotions of passion, romance, and depth. It was his Tchaikovsky's Piano Concerto No. 1, with its captivating melodies and powerful emotions that struck a chord within me. It was the pianist's joyful expression on stage that sparked my desire to connect with the piano. I wished that I could confidently step on stage and shine like him.

A Dream & Friend

Becoming a skilled pianist has always been my dream that I'll never give up. However, I also realized the meaning of music to me through the process. Piano is like a close accompany for me. We share emotions, love, and passion.

I believe obstacles will not deter me from pursuing music because of my love for it. Music is a significant part of my life, but more than that, it has cultivated my fortitude and persistence to achieve my goals. I am a classical music enthusiast, and I am proud of the person I have become.

Where words leave off, music begins.

Silence. The stagnant air contained excitement yet a feeling of nervousness. Hundreds of gazes focused on me. Sitting under the spotlight, I was the sole star within the extreme darkness. The jet-black Steinway & Sons piano in front of me shone with pride, waiting for my hands to deftly dance on its keyboard. I was passionate as if a fire was burning in my heart. Despite my apprehension, I confidently played the whole piece that I had practiced for two years. And it was the most glorious moment when I advanced to the final round of the Victory International Music Finals piano competition.



Music is the soundtrack of life.

I spent cumbersome years steadily building my foundation, but it did not dwindle my enthusiasm. In second grade, I auditioned for the Christmas concert for the first time. To everyone's surprise, I was the youngest student to be selected to perform. I struggled through the final days of practice until the minute before I stepped onto the stairs backstage. The only glimmer of light guided me to the magnificent piano. I clenched my fists, realizing that I could not see the audience within the darkness. Before fear overpowered my excited mind, I turned my gaze towards the shining black and gently placed my hands where they belonged. The music began.



Even though I have since experienced countless performances and have competed with highly skilled pianists, I am sometimes still afraid of being on stage. Nevertheless, every concert I participated in nourished and fostered my confidence. Every competition was a precious experience that propelled me to a higher level.

In music the passions enjoy themselves.

I have experienced a complete failure that became a dark chapter in my history. I forgot a piece in the middle of the performance and froze on stage. Despite feeling embarrassed and disappointed, I refused to let it define me. With a determination to complete the performance, I mustered up the courage, breathed in deeply, and continued playing from where I had failed. I wanted to place more value on focusing on the future.

I believe obstacles will not deter me from pursuing music because of my love for it. Music is a significant part of my life, but more than that, it has cultivated my fortitude and persistence to achieve my goals. Now, I even approach other aspects of my life with the same attitude. I am a classical music enthusiast, and I am proud of the person I have become.
